Blog

Featuring the strong voices of our artists on the issues and topics they care most about.

Responsible Art

by Samantha Naylor

How can we ensure that our work and artistic practice can simultaneously push boundaries while remaining conscious of our privileges/positionalites? What should be off limits and what can be explored respectfully?

Art During Covid

by Marcus Delaney

When the world was put on halt, artists found themselves in an interesting position of self-reflection and (for some) greater creative opportunity. Marcus Delaney explores some of the most prolific art that came out of the pandemic.

Friends in Theatre

by Davinder Singh

Making friends as an adult can be difficult, especially in a highly competitive industry with often little resources to go around. How can we grow a strong, friendly community while still reaching our individual artistic goals?

Staying Motivated

by Henry Castiglione

Sometimes the idea comes faster than the process of actually creating. How can we stay motivated in our art when costs of living continue to increase, there never seems to be enough time, and we still want to be able to experience fun and leisure?

Sustainable Art

by Janessa Sweeney

The world is only getting hotter, and although theatre artists are less likely to have gigantic carbon footprints, everyone has a responsibility to alter their practices in a way that best benefits the earth. How can we make sustainability a central focus in our art and creative process?

Pressing Stories

by Bilal Malhi

Have an idea for a story that’s been eating you alive? Don’t know where to even begin? Feel like your concept is too ambitious for the time and resources you possess? Bilal Malhi highlights some central ways to rework a vision that best suits your life. Don’t leave your good ideas out to dry!
